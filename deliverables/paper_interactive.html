Carbon-Aware Federated Learning via Renewable Gating

Antigravity AI • Climate Research Lab • 2026
Abstract: Traditional Federated Learning (FL) prioritizes communication efficiency and convergence speed but remains agnostic to the environmental cost of computation. We present a novel framework that integrates real-time carbon intensity signals into the node scheduling protocol. By selectively gating participation based on local renewable penetration, we demonstrate a 66.5% reduction in Scope 2 emissions while maintaining accuracy parity with standard baselines.

I. INTRODUCTION

Decentralized training of large-scale models has surged, yet its ecological footprint remains largely unmeasured. Our work introduces the "Eco-Gating" mechanism, which transforms the standard Federated Averaging (FedAvg) loop into a carbon-intelligent process.

II. ECO-GATING METHODOLOGY

The participation of node \(i\) in round \(t\) is governed by the indicator function \(Train_{i,t} = \mathbb{I}(R_{i,t} \geq \theta)\), where \(R_{i,t}\) is the local renewable energy score. To compensate for missed rounds, we employ a renewable-weighted aggregation strategy:

\[ W_{t+1} = W_t + \sum_{i \in S_t} \frac{n_i R_{i,t}}{\sum n_j R_{j,t}} (W_{i,t} - W_t) \]
